Mystery of Mt.Chiliad

Intro


Players have been attempting to discover the truth about the mysteries that reside beneath the Mount Chiliad ever since Grand Theft Auto 5 was first released. It turns out that Mt. Chiliad itself may not actually hold the key to interstellar travel, despite the fact that UFOs undoubtedly exist and the FIB does not want you to be aware of the existence of extraterrestrials.


Mt. Chiliad is home to an abandoned military bunker and missile silo, as revealed in the Doomsday Heist update. Of course, it's ultimately used to maybe end all life as we know it, but at least we got our long-awaited jetpacks. The bunker, which also houses the missile silo and warhead storage, is accessed through a secret cargo door that leads into a network of tunnels beneath the mountain.

The beginning of the search


One of the first points of interest for GTA conspiracy enthusiasts was a cryptic painting on the wall of the Cable Car station at the highest point of Mount Chiliad.The main map's striking resemblance to a pyramid's shaft and room layout raises the possibility of a hidden dungeon. A few tiny marks are also scattered over the picture. The enormous emblems of a cracking egg, a spaceship, and a humanoid form remain a fascinating puzzlement even though the meaning of many of the symbols within have been encrypted.
