How to build the best attacking formation

Putting in a defensive tackle that stops a scoring opportunity definitely comes with a lot of satisfaction, but in FIFA, there’s nothing quite like the exhilaration of moving up the pitch with the focus of getting the ball in the back of the net.

There are plenty of ways to attack in FIFA 23 and you can set your team up to align with your preferred style of play. Whether you prefer to emulate Manchester City‘s possession-heavy brand of football or Liverpool’s heavy metal direct play, there’s a style for every type of player.

Build up play

 1. Balanced

The team will maintain formation and structure. Players will only support and make runs when they think it’s the right time to do so. The team does not build play particularly fast or slow.

2. Slow Build Up

Less forward runs involved and more of a methodical approach. This tactic emphasizes a short passing game with a slower build-up.

3. Long Ball

Forwards will be ready for direct balls to be played behind the opposing back line. This attacking style often skips the midfield and flourishes with fast players who have excellent positioning.

4. Fast Build Up

The quickest way to move your team up the pitch and into direct scoring positions. High risk and high reward strategy that could leave you open to counterattack.

Chance Creation

 1. Balanced

Similar to Build Up play, a balanced approach for chance creation is neither fast nor slow when it comes to creating opportunities.

2. Possession

This tactic strengthens a short passing game to patiently wait for the chance to attack, but will rarely provide runs penetrating the opposing defensive line.

3. Direct Passing

Once the team enters the attacking zone while in possession, players will create chances by making runs for passes into space behind the opposing back line.

4. Forward Runs

This tactic pushes players forward deep into attacking areas, but if you lose possession of the ball you could find yourself open to a counterattack.
