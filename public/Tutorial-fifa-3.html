How to build the best defensive formation

The first thing you’ll have to consider when setting up your defensive tactics is the positioning of your back line. If your defensive line is equipped with a ton of pace, you could get away with playing a high line and applying maximum pressure on the opposing team.

If you’re not keen on leaving too much space between your defenders and goalkeeper then you can set your width and depth to be a little deeper. In addition, you’ll pair this positioning with one of the defensive styles below.

Keep in mind that your width and depth should correlate with a defensive style. For example, it would be counterintuitive to set up a high line only to enforce the Drop Back tactic on defense.

1. Width/Depth slider

With this slide, players can set the positioning of their defensive line. A high line is great for attacking but leaves you open to balls in behind, whereas a deep line is ideal for when you’re defending a lead.

2. Drop Back

This tactic will preserve your team shape and will offer your opponents more possession. Provides fewer attacking options when you do pick up the ball, but can frustrate your opponents.

3. Balanced

A balanced style where your team presses the ball to the middle of the pitch and your team’s shape is neutral.

4. Pressure on Heavy Touch

Your team preserves its shape until the opportunity to pressure appears. When the opposition creates a loose ball or has trouble controlling a difficult pass your players will pounce.

5. Press After Possession Loss

After your team loses possession of the ball, your teammates will press the ball for approximately 7 seconds. This style is taxing on stamina and could leave you exposed if you don’t win the ball back right

6. Constant Pressure

This tactic will constantly apply high pressure on opposing players but it drains your stamina the most.
